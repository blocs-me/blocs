## What Makes a Notion Widget "Minimalist"?

A minimalist Notion widget does one thing well and stays out of your way. That means no banner ads, no cluttered UI, no mandatory account creation, and no visual noise that pulls your eye away from your actual work. The best ones feel like they were designed for Notion, not bolted on from another app.

The practical checklist for minimalist widget design:

- **Clean typography** — readable at small sizes without being decorative
- **Neutral or themeable color palette** — should match light or dark Notion backgrounds
- **No branding clutter** — minimal or removable vendor logos
- **Instant load** — no splash screens or loading animations
- **Single function** — does one job, not five

## How to Embed a Minimalist Widget in Notion

1. Go to [blocs.me](https://blocs.me) and pick the widget you want.
2. Copy the embed URL (e.g., `https://blocs.me/pomodoro`).
3. In Notion, type `/embed` and select the Embed block.
4. Paste the URL and press Enter.
5. Resize the embed block to fit your layout.

That's the full setup. No account needed for free widgets. The widget loads inline with your page content — it doesn't open a new tab or redirect you anywhere.

## What to Look for When Choosing a Minimalist Notion Widget

Not every widget marketed as "clean" or "minimal" actually is. Here's what to check before embedding anything in your workspace:

- **Does it work without an account?** Widgets requiring sign-up create friction and can break if you're logged out.
- **Is the design consistent with other widgets from the same provider?** Mixing widgets from different sources usually produces a mismatched aesthetic.
- **Can you remove vendor branding?** A logo in the corner of every widget adds visual noise to your workspace.
- **Is it a subscription or a one-time purchase?** Subscriptions mean the widget disappears if you stop paying. Blocs Pro is a $17 one-time payment with lifetime access.
- **Does it update your data across devices?** Cloud sync (a Pro Blocs feature) ensures your habits and progress don't reset when you switch devices.
