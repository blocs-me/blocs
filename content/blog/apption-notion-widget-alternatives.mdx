## What Is Apption and Why Are People Looking for Alternatives?

Apption is a widget directory that lets Notion users browse and embed third-party widgets into their pages using iframes. It serves as an aggregator — linking out to widgets built by various developers rather than building them in-house. While it introduced many Notion users to the concept of embedded widgets, several pain points have pushed people to look elsewhere:

- **Widget reliability:** Because Apption links to third-party widgets, availability depends on whether those external services stay live. Widgets break when external developers abandon their projects.
- **Inconsistent quality:** Design and UX varies significantly from widget to widget, since they come from different sources.
- **Limited customization:** Most widgets in Apption's catalog offer little to no personalization — no themes, no goal-setting, no analytics.
- **Unclear pricing:** It's not always obvious which widgets are free, which require accounts, or which have hidden fees.

The Notion community has grown significantly, and users now expect more polished, reliable tools that feel native to their workspace.

## How to Switch from Apption to Blocs

Switching is straightforward because Blocs widgets are embedded the same way as any other iframe in Notion — through the **/embed** command.

1. Go to [blocs.me](https://blocs.me) and pick the widget you want (Pomodoro, Habit Tracker, Water Tracker, etc.)
2. Copy the embed URL for that widget (e.g., `https://blocs.me/pomodoro`)
3. Open your Notion page and type **/embed** in a new block
4. Paste the URL and press Enter — the widget loads live in your page
5. Resize the embed block to fit your layout

For a more detailed walkthrough, see the guide on [how to embed a Pomodoro timer in Notion](https://blocs.me/blog/how-to-embed-pomodoro-in-notion). The same steps apply to all Blocs widgets.
